Everything you need to know.

Do I keep my copyrights?
+
Yes, always. Davincii administers your rights — we never acquire or own them. You retain 100% copyright ownership of all your compositions at all times.
How do I get paid?
+
We render quarterly accounting statements and send payouts via ACH, PayPal, or Stripe. You can request payment from your artist portal once your balance exceeds $50.
What if I'm already registered with ASCAP or BMI?
+
Great — you should be. Davincii works alongside your existing PRO membership. We handle the publishing side and collect mechanical, digital performance, and neighboring rights your PRO may not cover.
What types of royalties do you collect?
+
Performing rights (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC), mechanical royalties (MLC), digital performance royalties (SoundExchange), and neighboring rights through international collection societies worldwide.
Can I leave at any time?
+
Yes. Either party can terminate with 30 days written notice. Following termination, we continue collecting royalties generated during your term for 12 months after, then remit your final balance.
Is there a minimum catalog size?
+
No. We work with artists at any stage — 1 song or 100. Every release deserves to be fully registered and earning.

Terms of Service v6 · Davincii LLC · New York, NY

We find your missing royalties and make sure you get paid. No upfront fees. No ownership grab. You keep 100% of your music.

By clicking "Accept" or completing onboarding, you're entering into an agreement with Davincii LLC ("Davincii"), a music publishing administration company based in New York. You are the "Artist" — or an Authorized Representative acting on the Artist's behalf.

Section 1. What Davincii Does For You

Davincii handles the administrative side of your publishing so you can focus on making music. That includes:

  • Registering your songs with PROs (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC, and others), mechanical agencies, and collection societies.
  • Hunting down royalties you've already earned but haven't been paid.
  • Collecting and sending you your share of every dollar we find.
  • Handling registration paperwork on your behalf.

We only administer your share of each song. We have zero authority over any co-writer's interest. We will never license your sync rights without your explicit written approval.

Your ownership is untouched. This agreement gives Davincii the exclusive right to administer your submitted catalog — nothing more. You keep 100% of your copyright, always.

Historical royalties. If we find and recover money you earned before signing up, we take our standard 15% and send you the rest.

1.1 Metadata

You agree to give us accurate song info: writer splits, co-writer details, ISWC and ISRC codes, and release dates. If we receive incomplete or inaccurate data, we can't be held responsible for any resulting gaps.

1.2 Limited Power of Attorney

To register your songs, we need a narrow power of attorney — strictly limited to filing registrations with PROs, mechanical agencies, and collection societies. This cannot be used to transfer ownership or encumber your rights, and it expires the moment this agreement ends.

1.3 Digital Performance Royalties

If you own or control the sound recording rights and haven't directed collection elsewhere, you can separately authorize Davincii to register with SoundExchange or similar organizations. That requires a signed Letter of Direction from you.

1.4 Exclusive Administration Grant

The rights granted to Davincii are exclusive. For each Composition you submit, Davincii is your sole and exclusive publishing administrator. This exclusivity applies only to Compositions you have specifically submitted — songs never submitted remain entirely your own.

1.5 Sub-Publishing

Davincii may appoint sub-publishers or affiliated collection societies to administer your Compositions in specific territories. Any such appointment: (a) does not reduce your 85% share; (b) does not transfer copyright; (c) is subject to the same sync licensing limitations. We will notify you within 30 days of any material sub-publishing arrangement.

1.6 Davincii's Service Warranties

Davincii warrants that it will: perform services with reasonable skill and care; register submitted Compositions within 60 days of receiving complete metadata; maintain accurate records; and promptly notify you if any registration is rejected or any issue materially affects collection.

Section 2. Your Catalog

Your catalog includes songs you've submitted, songs we've accepted, and in certain circumstances, songs Davincii submits on your behalf (Section 2.3). "Compositions" means any musical work you authored or co-authored. "Royalty Income" means publishing income: performance royalties (publisher's share), mechanical royalties, sync income, micro-sync income, and related publishing revenue. It does not include master recording income or label-side royalties.

2.1 Reversion Right

If Davincii fails to register a submitted Composition within 90 days of receiving complete metadata, and fails to cure within 30 days of written notice, you may remove that Composition. Upon removal: (a) Davincii's rights terminate immediately; (b) Davincii will de-register the Composition; (c) royalties already collected remain subject to the 15%/85% split.

2.2 Unmatched Royalties

Unmatched royalty income is held for 24 months while Davincii makes reasonable efforts to identify the source. Unmatched Income is reported separately in your statement and is never retained by Davincii without your written agreement.

2.3 Davincii-Initiated Submissions

Davincii may identify and submit Compositions on your behalf, including: (a) unregistered or misregistered works found through gap analysis; (b) back catalog not submitted at onboarding; (c) Compositions generating unclaimed royalty income in your name. All royalties collected are treated identically to the rest of your catalog.

Section 3. Fees — Simple and Transparent

No upfront costs.

3.1 Administration Fee

Davincii retains 15% of all royalties collected on your behalf. You receive 85% of everything we bring in.

3.2 Co-Writer Registration Fee

When a song has co-writers requiring registration work, a one-time fee of $100.00 per co-writer per song applies. Never charged without your prior written approval.

3.3 Accounting and Payment

Every quarter, within 45 days, you receive a clear statement. If your balance is under $50, it rolls to next quarter. We pay via ACH, wire, PayPal, or another method we agree on.

3.4 Your Audit Right

Once per year, with 30 days' notice, you may audit our records at your expense. Statements are final if no written objection is raised within 180 days.

Section 4. Term and What Happens When You Leave

This agreement runs for one year, renewing automatically unless either party gives 30 days' notice. Either party can exit early only for a material breach not cured within 30 days.

After you leave. For 12 months after this agreement ends, Davincii continues collecting royalties earned during the term. Our 15% fee applies only during that window. After 12 months, Davincii has no further right to collect or take fees. Final accounting within 60 days of termination.

Section 5. Your Promises to Us

You confirm that: (a) you have authority to enter this agreement; (b) you own or co-own the songs you submit; (c) your songs don't infringe anyone else's rights. Both parties agree to keep each other's private financial and business information confidential during and for three years after the agreement.

Section 6. The Legal Stuff

6.1 Liability. Our liability is capped at fees retained in the 12 months before the claim. Cap doesn't apply to gross negligence or willful misconduct.

6.2 Force Majeure. Neither party is liable for delays caused by things outside our control.

6.3 Disputes. Mediation in New York first; binding arbitration under AAA Commercial Rules if mediation fails within 30 days. New York law governs.

Section 7. Everything Else

7.1 Assignment. Davincii may transfer to an affiliate or successor with 10 days' notice. You may not transfer without written consent.

7.2 Notices. By email (with read confirmation) or certified mail.

7.3 Full Agreement. This is the complete agreement. Changes require written sign-off from both parties.

7.4 Severability. If any part is unenforceable, the rest stays. Electronic signatures are valid.

7.5 Metadata Delivery Format. You agree to deliver metadata in a format reasonably requested by Davincii. Timelines for registration (Section 1.6) and reversion (Section 2.1) begin once complete metadata is received.
